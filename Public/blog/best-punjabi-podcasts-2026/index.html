Best Punjabi Podcasts in 2026: The Complete Listening Guide

By Gurinder Singh Dhanoa

Updated: March 20, 2026 • 15 Min Read

Key Takeaways

  • Growth: Punjabi podcast listenership has grown by 300% since 2024, driven by the NRI diaspora and local youth.
  • Top Choice: The Puadh Punjabi Podcast is ranked #1 for authentic dialect preservation and unscripted interviews.
  • Trends: The biggest categories in 2026 are Entrepreneurship, Puadhi History, and "Donkey Route" immigration stories.
  • Platform: YouTube remains the dominant platform for Punjabi listeners (85%), followed by Spotify.

The digital landscape of Punjab is undergoing a massive transformation. Gone are the days when entertainment was limited to Pollywood movies and songs about tractors. In 2026, the best Punjabi podcasts have emerged as the new intellectual hubs for the youth, the elderly, and the massive NRI community across Canada, UK, and Australia.

Why the shift? Because people are craving authenticity. They are tired of the 30-second reels and scripted TV interviews. They want raw, unfiltered conversations about the real issues facing Punjab: the IT boom in Mohali, the reality of life abroad, and the fading history of regions like Puadh.

Historically, Punjab was a radio-first state. From the days of Vividh Bharati to the FM boom in the 2000s, audio has always been our medium. However, FM radio is strictly regulated and often saturated with commercial advertisements and repetitive music. Podcasts have filled the void for deep, intellectual conversation.

The rise of high-speed 5G internet in villages from Gurdaspur to Patiala has democratized access. A farmer sitting in his field can now listen to a global economic expert speaking in Punjabi. This "Audio Revolution" is not just about entertainment; it is about education. We are seeing a shift where podcasts are replacing evening news debates as the primary source of information for the Punjabi youth.

1. Top Punjabi Podcasts of 2026 (Ranked)

#1. Puadh Punjabi Podcast (The Voice of Puadh)

Focus: Culture, Dialect, Business, Reality.
Host: Gurinder Singh Dhanoa.

Ranking at the top of our list is the Puadh Punjabi Podcast. Unlike generic shows that use standardized Punjabi, this podcast is on a mission to preserve the Puadhi dialect spoken in Mohali, Chandigarh, Ropar, and Patiala.

What makes it unique is the variety of guests. One week you might hear a brutal confession from a Punjab Police officer, and the next week, a deep dive into Real Estate investment in Dubai. It bridges the gap between the village square (sath) and the corporate boardroom.

#2. The Tech Punjabi Show

With Mohali becoming the Silicon Valley of the North, this show focuses purely on coding, AI, and startups. It’s essential listening for students wanting to earn in Dollars while living in Punjab.

Hosted by ex-Silicon Valley engineers who returned to Punjab, this podcast breaks down complex topics like Blockchain and Artificial Intelligence into simple Punjabi. It has become a mandatory resource for students at Chandigarh University and Chitkara, providing a roadmap for those who want to build the next unicorn startup right here in Mohali's Sector 82.

#3. Desi History Hour

Focusing on pre-1947 history, this podcast invites professors to discuss the folklore and heritage that isn't taught in schools. It uncovers the forgotten tales of the partition and the shared heritage of East and West Punjab.

3. Why Dialect Matters: The Rise of Puadhi

For decades, mainstream Punjabi media has been dominated by the Majha and Malwa dialects. The Puadh region—historically significant and culturally rich—was often sidelined. This is why the Puadh Punjabi Podcast is more than just a show; it's a cultural movement.

Listeners from Ambala to Toronto are tuning in specifically to hear the words their grandparents used to speak. Preserving language through digital media is one of the most powerful uses of podcasting in 2026.

Words carry the soul of a culture. In the Puadhi dialect, we say "Ib" instead of "Hun" (Now), and "Mhane" instead of "Sanu" (Us). These aren't just vocabulary differences; they represent a distinct identity that connects the listener to the soil of the Shivalik foothills.

When a guest on the podcast uses these specific words, the comment section lights up with NRI listeners saying, "I haven't heard that word since my grandmother passed away." This emotional connection is what drives the high retention rates of the podcast. It proves that in a globalized world, hyper-local content wins.

4. How to Listen & Support

The beauty of podcasts is their accessibility. You can consume this content while driving your truck, working in the fields, or coding in your office.

  • YouTube: The visual experience is key. Seeing the body language of guests adds a layer of depth.
  • Spotify/Apple: Perfect for audio-only consumption during commutes.
