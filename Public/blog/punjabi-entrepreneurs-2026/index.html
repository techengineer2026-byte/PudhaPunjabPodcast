Top Punjabi Entrepreneurs — Success Stories from the Puadh Region 2026

March 18, 2026  |  5 Min Read  |  Entrepreneurship

Punjab is changing. The narrative is shifting from "Udta Punjab" to "Rising Punjab," driven by a new generation of entrepreneurs who are redefining what it means to do business in the North. While agriculture remains the backbone of the state, the Puadh region—encompassing Mohali, Chandigarh, Panchkula, and surrounding areas—has become a hotbed for startups, service industries, and creative ventures.

In 2026, we are seeing more young Punjabis staying in the Tricity area to build empires rather than rushing abroad. From Mohali's Phase 8B Industrial Area to the cafes of Chandigarh, the entrepreneurial spirit is alive and thriving.

The New Face of Punjabi Business

What makes this era unique is the blend of traditional Punjabi values—hard work (mehnat) and resilience—combined with modern technology and global ambition. The entrepreneurs we interview on the Puadh Punjabi Podcast aren't just building businesses; they are building communities.

Here are 5 inspiring Punjabi entrepreneurs we’ve had the privilege of hosting at our Mohali studio, whose stories define the spirit of 2026.

1. Anshul Bansal (Episode 23)

Anshul Bansal represents the strategic side of Punjab's growth. In his conversation with us, he broke down the complexities of modern infrastructure and business scalability in the region. His journey proves that with a clear vision and architectural precision, you can build a legacy right here in the Tricity.

2. Vikram (Episode 28)

Vikram’s story is one of pure grit. Rising through the competitive landscape of the service industry, he shared the unvarnished truth about what it takes to acquire and retain clients in North India. His episode is a masterclass for anyone looking to understand the psychology of the Punjabi consumer.

3. Akash (Episode 18)

The youth of Punjab are not waiting for permission to succeed. Akash, one of our younger guests, brought an infectious energy to the studio, discussing how digital tools are leveling the playing field. His success highlights how the younger generation in Mohali is pivoting from traditional jobs to the digital creator economy.

4. Jobanjeet (Episode 15)

Jobanjeet bridges the gap between Punjab and the global stage. His episode touched on the critical themes of education and the mindset shift required to compete internationally. For many aspiring entrepreneurs looking to expand beyond state borders, Jobanjeet’s advice was nothing short of gold.

5. Gurjeet Kamboj (Episode 7)

You cannot talk about Punjabi success without acknowledging our roots. Gurjeet Kamboj joined us early in our journey to discuss the intersection of culture and commerce. His story reminds us that staying true to your heritage—and your dialect—can actually be a massive competitive advantage in today's homogenized market.

Conclusion

The Puadh region is no longer just a transit point; it is a destination for innovation. These five entrepreneurs are just the tip of the iceberg. At Puadh Punjabi Podcast, we are committed to documenting this history as it happens, one episode at a time.
