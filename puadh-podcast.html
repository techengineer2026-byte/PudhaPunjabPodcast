What is the Puadh Region?

Puadh is a historic region of Punjab extending from the Satluj River to the Ghaggar River. It includes:

  • Chandigarh - The capital city (Union Territory)
  • Mohali - The tech hub of Punjab
  • Ropar (Rupnagar) - Ancient historical city
  • Patiala - The royal city
  • Ambala - The strategic location
  • Parts of Haryana and Himachal Pradesh

Unlike Majha (central Punjab) and Malwa (southern Punjab), Puadh has its own distinct culture, history, and language variations.

The Puadhi Dialect

Puadhi is a unique dialect of Punjabi language spoken in the Puadh region. It's distinct from other Punjabi dialects with special vocabulary, pronunciation, and cultural expressions.

Characteristics:

  • Unique pronunciation patterns
  • Regional vocabulary
  • Cultural idioms
  • Rich heritage

Compared to Other Dialects:

  • Different from Majhi (central)
  • Different from Malwai (southern)
  • Different from Doabi
  • Rarely heard outside region
Our Mission

The Voice of the Puadh Region

The Puadh Punjabi Podcast was born from a simple yet powerful necessity: to give a global platform to the often-overlooked Puadh region of Punjab. While Majha and Malwa often dominate the cultural narrative, our region—stretching from the Satluj to the Ghaggar river—holds a distinct history, a unique Puadhi dialect, and an economic vibrancy that needed a voice.

Hosted by Prince, a proud local who understands the pulse of the land, this podcast strips away the glitz of scripted interviews to deliver raw, unfiltered conversations. What makes our stories unique is the "Puadhi" flavor—honest, direct, and deeply rooted in community values.
